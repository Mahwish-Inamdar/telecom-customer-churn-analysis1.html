Telecom Customer Churn Analysis¶

Objective¶

The primary objective of this analysis is to understand why customers are leaving (churning) from a telecom company.
By examining customer data, I aim to identify the key drivers behind churn, uncover patterns, and provide actionable insights that can help reduce customer turnover and improve retention.

Business Problem¶

Customer churn poses a significant challenge for telecom companies.
Losing customers not only affects revenue but also increases the cost of acquiring new ones.
Retaining existing customers is more cost-effective than attracting new ones—so understanding the reasons behind churn is critical for maintaining long-term profitability and customer satisfaction.

Key Questions to Explore¶

  • What percentage of customers are churning?
  • Are there specific customer groups (by contract, service type, etc.) more likely to churn?
  • Do monthly charges or other service-related factors influence churn behavior?
  • What business actions can be taken to proactively reduce churn?

Summary of Insights¶

1. Churn Rate:¶

  • Approximately 26.6% of customers are churning, which is a moderate but concerning figure for the business.
  • A high churn rate indicates potential revenue loss and increased customer acquisition costs, both of which directly affect profitability.

2. Churn by Contract Type:¶

  • Customers on month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate, which suggests that short-term commitments may lead to dissatisfaction or indecision about staying with the service.
  • 1-year and 2-year contracts show significantly lower churn rates, indicating that customers with longer-term commitments are generally more satisfied and more likely to stay.

3. Churn by Internet Service:¶

  • Fiber optic users have the highest churn rate. This may be due to factors such as service reliability, pricing, or perceived value of the service.
  • DSL users tend to be more loyal, possibly due to better satisfaction with service quality or lower expectations.
  • Non-internet users (those without internet services) have shown no churn, which could imply these customers are either highly loyal or have specific needs (e.g., landline services).

4. Churn and Monthly Charges:¶

  • Customers with higher monthly charges (₹70-₹100) are more likely to churn, suggesting they might feel they are not receiving adequate value for the cost.
  • Customers who stay tend to have lower monthly charges, indicating that pricing plays a significant role in customer retention and satisfaction.

Recommendations¶

1. Reevaluate Contract Offerings:¶

  • Consider offering longer-term contracts with added incentives (e.g., discounts or exclusive benefits) to reduce churn among month-to-month customers.
  • Provide flexible contract options and discounts to encourage customers to commit long-term without feeling “locked in.”

2. Enhance Fiber Optic Service:¶

  • Investigate the reasons behind the higher churn rate among fiber optic customers, focusing on aspects like service quality, pricing concerns, and customer support.
  • Introduce loyalty perks, such as service improvements, discounts, or premium support, to retain fiber optic users and increase their satisfaction.

3. Monitor Monthly Charges and Pricing:¶

  • Higher charges are correlated with churn, so it’s essential to review the pricing strategy for customers in the higher charge bracket.
  • Offer tailored pricing plans or discounted packages for high-paying customers to ensure they perceive value for money.
  • For price-sensitive customers, introduce more budget-friendly plans to prevent them from feeling the service is not worth the cost.

4. Improve Customer Onboarding and Support:¶

  • Enhance the onboarding process for month-to-month customers by clearly outlining the value of long-term commitments and offering exclusive deals for contract extensions.
  • Introduce a loyalty program that rewards customers for renewing or extending their contracts, fostering a sense of reward and belonging.

5. Targeted Retention Campaigns Based on Service Type:¶

  • For DSL users, consider introducing premium features (e.g., faster speeds or additional benefits) as a way to enhance their experience and foster loyalty.
  • For fiber optic users, consider implementing a feedback loop where issues are directly addressed, along with enhancing customer support to resolve service concerns promptly.

Conclusion¶

This churn analysis reveals key drivers of customer churn and provides actionable insights to improve retention:

  • Contract Types: A stronger focus on promoting long-term contracts can help reduce churn by creating more committed customer relationships.
  • Pricing: Adjusting pricing plans and introducing discounts for higher-paying customers can address the issue of value perception and reduce churn.
  • Service Quality: Enhancing fiber optic services and addressing pricing concerns will significantly improve customer retention and overall satisfaction.

By implementing these targeted recommendations, the company can reduce churn, enhance customer satisfaction, and drive long-term profitability.
